Autonomous Planning

A Planner is used when a goal is too big for an AI to solve in one go. Instead, it breaks the goal into a "To-Do List" and checks them off one by one.

Why use a Planner?

If you ask an AI to "Write a 50-page book," it might fail or get confused. A Planner would break it down:

  1. Research the topic.
  2. Write Chapter 1.
  3. Write Chapter 2... and so on.

The 4 Pieces of a Planner:

  • 1. The Planner (LLM): Takes the big goal and outputs a list of smaller steps.
  • 2. The Filter (Variable Extractor): Grabs just the "To-Do List" from the AI's response.
  • 3. The Manager (Sequential Batch): Hands each task from the list to the next node, one at a time.
  • 4. The Expert (Sub-Workflow): A specialized workflow that knows how to execute a single task.
